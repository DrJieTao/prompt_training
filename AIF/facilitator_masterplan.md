# SECTION 3: RTCF MINI-LESSON (0:03–0:08)

## 3.1 The Formula

| Element | What It Does | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **R – Role** | Defines WHO the AI is | "Act as a Senior VC Associate at a growth-stage fund" |
| **T – Task** | Specifies WHAT to do | "Create a 6-slide investment memo" |
| **C – Context** | Provides the STAKES | "For Monday's IC meeting; our fund focuses on B2B SaaS with $3M+ ARR" |
| **F – Format** | Dictates HOW it looks | "Markdown outline with slides for Executive Summary, Market, Risks, Next Steps" |

## 3.2 The "Lazy Prompt" vs. RTCF Prompt

**Show this comparison on screen:**

| Lazy Prompt | RTCF Prompt |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Summarize this research into a deck" | "Act as a Senior VC Associate. Create a 6-slide investment memo for Monday's IC meeting. Our fund focuses on B2B SaaS with $3M+ ARR. Output as a Markdown outline with slides for Executive Summary, Market Opportunity, Competitive Landscape, Technical Moats, Red Flags, and Next Steps." |

> "See the difference? The lazy prompt gives you a coin flip. The RTCF prompt gives you a professional starting point."

## 3.3 Introducing Constraints

> "But RTCF alone isn't enough. We add a Constraints section—the DO's and DON'Ts that prevent the AI from making common mistakes."

**Show example Constraints:**

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|-------|---------|
| Only cite data from the provided research | Do not invent market figures |
| Flag any contradictory data points | Do not use "revolutionary" or "game-changing" |
| List 3 "Unresolved Questions" for diligence | Do not provide a recommendation—only surface evidence |

## 3.4 Transition to Demo 1

## 5.5 The Prompts (Quick Reference)

### Phase A: Interview Pattern

```
You are a Strategic Communications Advisor helping a VC investor prepare 
a visual summary for a portfolio company board meeting.

Before generating any output, ask me exactly 3 questions, ONE AT A TIME. 
Focus on: audience, purpose, and any ambiguous metrics. Wait for my 
response before asking the next question.

Here are my notes:
[PASTE IC NOTES]
```

### Phase B: Generate First Draft

```
Based on my answers, generate a structured text block as clean bullet 
points organized into: THE PROBLEM, COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE, POSITIONING, 
KEY METRICS, and KEY RISK. Keep each bullet ≤15 words.
```

### Phase C: Persona Review

```
Review this draft as TWO personas:

PERSONA 1: The Skeptical GP – Wants risks stated clearly, metrics verified, 
no spin. Provide 2-3 critiques and 1-2 improvements.

PERSONA 2: The Portfolio CEO – Wants to feel heard, needs clarity on next 
steps, sensitive to framing. Provide 2-3 critiques and 1-2 improvements.

Synthesize the top 3 improvements to make.
```

### Phase D: Refine

```
Incorporate the top 3 improvements identified above and regenerate the 
structured text block. Maintain the plain-text bullet format.
```

### Phase E: Napkin

No prompt—paste plain text directly into Napkin → Click "Spark" → Select style → Polish → Export.

## 5.7 Key Talking Points

### Introducing the Interview Pattern

> "Notice the AI is asking US questions. This is the Interview Pattern. Instead of guessing your context, it extracts it. Three questions, maximum signal."

### The Ambiguity Catch

> "Did you see that? It asked about the churn metric. '18% last quarter' is ambiguous. The AI caught it before we embarrassed ourselves in front of LPs."

### Introducing the Persona Review

> "This is the Persona Review Pattern. We're asking the AI to critique its own work through two lenses: a skeptical GP and the founder who'll receive this."

### The Tension

> "Notice the GP wants more risk disclosure. The CEO wants softer framing. These are real tensions you navigate every day. The AI surfaced them before the meeting."

### The Meta-Lesson

> "Demo 1 showed you RTCF + Constraints. Demo 2 shows you Interview + Persona Review. You're not writing prompts anymore—you're directing workflows."

## 5.8 Pitfall Warnings

### Warning 1: Interview Fatigue (After Q3)

> ⚠️ "Three questions is the sweet spot. More than five and you've lost the efficiency gain."

### Warning 2: Echo Chamber (During Persona Review)

> ⚠️ "Choose personas with tension—like a skeptical GP and an optimistic founder. That tension is where the insight lives."

### Warning 3: Refinement Loop (After Phase D)

> ⚠️ "One refinement pass is usually enough. Perfectionism is the enemy of done."

### Warning 4: Garbage Text, Garbage Visual (Before Napkin)

> ⚠️ "Napkin is only as good as the text you feed it. That's why we spent time structuring the content first."
